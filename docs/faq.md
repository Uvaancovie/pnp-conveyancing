## These are the FAQ page that must be shown with accordion style.

1. ## What is conveyancing?
Conveyancing is the legal process by which ownership of immovable property is
transferred from a seller to a purchaser. It includes drafting and lodging documents,
ensuring compliance with statutory requirements, managing payments, and registering the
transfer at the Deeds Office.

2. ## Who appoints the conveyancing attorney?
The seller usually appoints the transferring attorney.
However, depending on the transaction, there may also be:
• A bond registration attorney (appointed by the bank granting the home loan),
and/or
• A bond cancellation attorney (appointed by the seller’s bank to cancel an existing
bond).

3. ## How long does a property transfer take?
On average, a transfer takes 8–12 weeks from date of sale, subject to:
• Bond approval timelines
• Clearance certificates (rates and levies)
• FICA compliance
• Signing of documents
• Deeds Office turnaround times
Delays most often arise from incomplete information or outstanding compliance items.

4. ## What costs are involved in conveyancing?
Common costs include:
• Transfer duty (if applicable)
• Conveyancing (transfer) fees
• Deeds Office fees
• Bond registration or cancellation costs (if applicable)

• Rates and levy clearance figures
A detailed cost estimate is usually provided upfront.

5. ## What is transfer duty?
Transfer duty is a tax payable to SARS on property purchases above the applicable
threshold, unless the transaction is VAT-rated.
It must be paid before transfer can be lodged.

6. ##  What documents will I need to provide?
Typical requirements include:
• Certified copy of ID or passport
• Proof of address (not older than 3 months)
• Proof of marital status (and ANC, if applicable)
• Income tax number
• FICA declarations and supporting documents
Additional documents may be required depending on the transaction.

7. ## What is FICA and why is it required?
FICA (Financial Intelligence Centre Act) requires attorneys to verify the identity and address
of clients to prevent fraud, money laundering, and financial crime.
Transfer cannot proceed without full FICA compliance.

8. ## When do I sign the transfer documents?
Transfer documents are usually signed early in the process, once draft documents are
prepared.
These are signed at the conveyancer’s offices (or remotely, where permitted).
